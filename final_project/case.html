Case Study: Redmond Massage

Analyze

To begin, if the client has a webpage they would like their new one to be based off of, or have certain ideas for the design or content, we will go over those together. This allows for the identification of content to keep, content to change, and the clients idea of what the finished webpage should look like.

Wireframe

Next, I will draw up a wireframe for each and every one of the webpages that the client would like. They will look like the one above and show the layout of the pages, as well as the basic idea for what content goes where. These, along with color scheme ideas and sample content allow for the client to give me feedback on what differences they would like to see, and me to make those changes before creating the initial website.

Feedback

In this step, I will present both the updated wireframes, and style choices, color pallettes, font options, and more in a powerpoint to the client. The client may make suggestions or give me more content/ideas for the pages in question. This gives the client one more opportunity to make changes to the design and to input their own creativity before the webpages are made for them.

Create

Lastly, based on all the feedback from the client, the wireframes drawn up by me, and the original content/ideas provided by the client, I will create finalized webpages with every aspect fully functional and upload these as a final product to either the client or the web depending on what is requested.
